How to Read an Analysis Report ​

Investment Feed | 2025.01.07

Analysis reports are crucial resources for making investment decisions. While they may seem overwhelming to beginners due to the volume of information and technical jargon, when utilized effectively, they can provide valuable investment ideas and insights into market trends. This article provides guidance on how to read and utilize analysis reports.

1️⃣ Why Should You Read an Analysis Report? ​

Analysis reports are highly beneficial to investors for the following reasons:

🧑‍🏫 Gain Expert Insights ​

  • Analysis reports are written by analysts who conduct in-depth research on companies and industries. These reports provide insights that individual investors may find difficult to access on their own due to the analysts' expertise and deep understanding of the market.

⏰ Save Time and Effort ​

  • Conducting your own analysis of companies and industries requires significant time and effort. Analysis reports summarize vast amounts of data, allowing you to acquire information more efficiently.

💡 Discover Investment Ideas ​

  • Reports not only analyze specific companies and industries but also highlight major market trends and investment opportunities. They can help you uncover new ideas or refine strategies for your current portfolio.

⛔️ Identify Risks in Advance ​

  • Reports address risk factors that should be considered when making investment decisions. This helps you anticipate and prepare for potential risks.

💹 Understand Market Consensus ​

  • Comparing reports from various sources reveals the market's collective view of a particular company or industry, known as market consensus. This understanding is crucial for predicting stock price trends and making strategic investment decisions.

2️⃣ Understanding the Structure of an Analysis Report ​

Analysis reports are generally structured and categorized as follows:

📑 Structure of an Analysis Report ​

Analysis reports are typically organized as follows:

💯 Investment Opinion and Target Price ​

  • This section summarizes the analyst’s key opinions, providing the investment appeal and target price of the stock.

🏭 Industry and Company Analysis ​

  • It analyzes industry trends, individual company financial data, market positioning, and growth potential.

🧐 Risk Factors ​

  • Potential risks that need to be considered when investing are outlined here.

🏷️ Valuation ​

  • This section evaluates the company’s value through methods and metrics, helping to determine whether the current stock price is reasonable.

🗂️ Types of Analysis Reports ​

Analysis reports are categorized into various types depending on their purpose and content:

🏢 Company Reports ​

  • These reports focus on individual companies and typically include:
    • Company overview
    • Financial performance analysis
    • Key products and services
    • Investment points and risks

🏗️ Industry Reports ​

  • These cover trends, key players, and competitive dynamics within a specific industry. They are helpful for understanding the growth potential and risks of the industry. Additionally, they provide insights not only for stock investments but also for related funds and exchange-traded funds (ETFs).

🚨 Issue Reports ​

  • These address specific issues that may affect the market, such as interest rate changes, exchange rate fluctuations, and regulatory shifts, often related to macroeconomic factors.

🔭 Market Outlook Reports ​

  • These analyze the overall flow of the securities market and the outlook for major sectors. They comprehensively cover markets such as stocks, bonds, and commodities, providing guidance for asset allocation strategies.

3️⃣ Key Points to Focus on in an Analysis Report ​

You don’t need to read the entire report from start to finish. To read efficiently, focus on the following points.

❶ Investment Opinion and Target Price ​

This is the conclusion of the report and serves as a key indicator to assess whether the company is worth investing in. However, remember that this opinion is not always accurate, so interpret it cautiously.

Investment Opinion

  • Investment opinions are typically divided into Buy, Hold, and Sell.
    • Buy: Indicates the stock should be purchased.
    • Sell: Indicates the stock should be sold.
    • Hold: Suggests there is no need to buy additional shares, but holding the current shares is fine.

Target Price

  • The target price is the projected price that the stock is expected to reach based on the analysis.
  • The gap between the target price and the current price is called the disparity rate, and investors sometimes decide whether to invest based on this rate.

❷ Earnings Outlook and Key Growth Drivers ​

The company’s Earnings Outlook is a crucial criterion for deciding whether to invest. In particular, understanding the Growth Drivers is essential.

  • Examples: Launch of new products, expansion into foreign markets, cost reduction effects, etc.

❸ Valuation and Market Assessment ​

Evaluate whether the current stock price is reasonable by checking valuation metrics. Also, consider Peer Analysis by comparing the company with its industry peers.

1. Earnings Per Share (EPS) ​

EPS: This represents the company’s net income per share.

  • Calculated by dividing net income by the total number of outstanding shares.
  • Higher EPS indicates a stock with greater investment value.

2. Price-to-Earnings Ratio (PER) ​

PER: This is the current stock price divided by EPS.

  • It indicates how many times the stock price is compared to the company’s earnings per share.
  • A higher PER compared to industry peers suggests the stock may be overvalued, while a lower PER suggests undervaluation.

3. Return on Equity (ROE) ​

ROE: This is calculated by dividing net income by shareholder equity.

  • It shows how much profit the company has generated with its equity.
  • Higher ROE is generally better.

4. EV/EBITDA ​

This indicates the time it would take to recover the investment cost.

  • EV: Enterprise Value, which represents the cost to acquire the company.
  • EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.
  • EV/EBITDA is calculated by dividing EV by EBITDA, showing how many years it would take to recover the investment if the company were acquired.

❹ Risk Factors ​

Always examine the Risk Factors that may be hidden behind optimistic projections. Unexpected variables can significantly affect investment outcomes.

4️⃣ Where to Access Analysis Reports ​

🟩 Naver Financial Research (https://finance.naver.com/research/) ​

Naver Financial Research is a platform that aggregates analysis reports from major brokerage firms. It allows you to compare various analysts' opinions through company-specific reports.

🗞️ Hankyung Consensus (https://markets.hankyung.com/consensus) ​

Operated by the Korea Economic Daily, Hankyung Consensus gathers analysis reports from multiple brokerages in one place.
Its main advantage is the ability to view the latest reports and brokerage-specific target prices at a glance.

Additionally, you can access reports through individual brokerage websites or trading platforms, so make the most of these resources.

5️⃣ Tips for Utilizing Reports ​

⚖️ Compare Various Reports ​

Don't rely solely on a single brokerage's report. Compare opinions from multiple brokerages. Analysts may have differing views even on the same company.

👩‍🏫 Check Analyst Credibility ​

Compare past reports with actual outcomes to assess the reliability of the analyst's predictions. Some analysts may consistently lean toward optimism or conservatism.

📊 Understand Market Trends ​

Analysis reports provide not only individual company insights but also information about the broader market. Use them to understand macro-economic trends and industry patterns.

6️⃣ Points to Keep in Mind ​

Analysis reports are reference materials and should not form the sole basis for your investment decisions. They may have the following limitations:

  • Analysts' subjective judgments may be included.
  • Short-term market fluctuations might not be reflected in the reports.
  • Reports may occasionally carry overly optimistic views about specific companies or industries.

Therefore, when reading reports, always base your judgment on your own investment philosophy and objectives.


💡 3-Point Summary

  • Proper use of analysis reports can significantly aid investment decisions.
  • Actively utilize Naver Financial Research and Hankyung Consensus to access diverse information and improve your investment judgment skills.
  • Continuously read and analyze reports to build your investment knowledge, paving the way for successful investing.
