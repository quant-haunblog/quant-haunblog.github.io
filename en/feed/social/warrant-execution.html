Warrant execution refers to the act of law enforcement agencies carrying out actions such as arrest, detention, and search and seizure based on warrants issued by the court. This process maintains a balance between the rights of suspects and public legal order, proceeding according to the procedures stipulated by law.

A warrant is a document issued by a court granting permission for law enforcement to perform specific actions, playing a crucial role in procedures such as forced investigations or arrests. Warrant execution is a vital process that harmonizes the authority of the judiciary and the fundamental rights of citizens.

Neutral Judgment by the Court

  • Warrants are issued by the judiciary to prevent abuse of authority by law enforcement agencies. Issuance is based on neutral and objective standards, requiring sufficient evidence of the alleged crime.

After a warrant is issued, law enforcement agencies execute it. This process includes the following steps:

  • Prior Notification: Inform the subject of the purpose and content of the warrant during execution.
  • Execution Method: Use the minimum force necessary to execute the warrant as prescribed by law.
  • Evidence Securing: For search and seizure, confirm and properly secure evidence.
