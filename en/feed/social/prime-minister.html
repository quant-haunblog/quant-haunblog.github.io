🧑‍🏫 Prime Minister ​

Today's Social Headlines | 2024.12.27

📌 Impeachment Motion Against Acting President Han Duck-soo: The First in Constitutional History ​

Prime Minister Han Duck-soo, acting as President, faces the first-ever impeachment vote against an acting president in Korea’s constitutional history. The impeachment motion cites multiple reasons, including the refusal to appoint a Constitutional Court justice.

The opposition party claims that the acting president’s decisions violated the Constitution and laws, adopting a firm stance. The ruling party strongly opposes the motion, escalating political tensions to their peak. If the impeachment is passed, the acting presidency will undergo significant changes once again.

1️⃣ Definition ​

The Prime Minister is the highest administrative officer in South Korea, tasked with assisting the President, overseeing and supervising ministries, and coordinating national affairs comprehensively, as stipulated by the Constitution. In the event of the President's vacancy or inability to perform duties, the Prime Minister assumes the role of Acting President to ensure continuity in governance.

Key Roles ​

Prime Minister's Key Roles

1. Assisting the President ​

The Prime Minister supports the President in ensuring smooth governance. They execute presidential policies and, when necessary, act on behalf of the President in managing state affairs.

2. Presiding Over Cabinet Meetings ​

As Vice Chair of the Cabinet, the Prime Minister presides over meetings in the President’s absence. These meetings are crucial for deliberating and deciding on key policies, laws, and budgets.

3. Coordinating the Executive Branch ​

The Prime Minister coordinates the work of ministries, provides directions for effective policy implementation, and resolves conflicts or mediates agreements between ministries when necessary.

4. Supervision and Direction ​

The Prime Minister oversees the operations of central administrative agencies and can issue directives to ensure consistency and efficiency in governance.

5. Personnel Management ​

Under the President’s authority, the Prime Minister assists in appointing key government officials, recommending suitable candidates, and supervising their roles in national governance.

6. Collaboration with the National Assembly ​

The Prime Minister addresses inquiries from the National Assembly, explains government policies, and seeks legislative cooperation. They actively participate in parliamentary activities, including audits, to represent the government’s stance.

7. Crisis Management ​

In times of national crises or emergencies, the Prime Minister represents the government, coordinates measures, and takes prompt action to ensure national security and public safety.

2️⃣ Principles and Legal Framework ​

📖 Constitution Article 86 ​

  • Article 86: The Prime Minister assists the President, oversees the ministries, and supports the Chair of the Cabinet.
  • Article 71: In cases where the President is incapacitated or unable to perform duties, the Prime Minister assumes the role of Acting President.

📖 Government Organization Act ​

  • Article 15: The Prime Minister, under the President’s direction, supervises and coordinates the work of ministries and serves as Vice Chair of the Cabinet.
  • Article 26: If an Acting President is impeached, the role is sequentially transferred to the Deputy Prime Minister for Economic Affairs.

📖 Historical Context ​

  • The Prime Minister was established as an independent administrative authority to counterbalance presidential power and maintain equilibrium within the executive branch. This structure incorporates elements of a parliamentary system within South Korea’s governance framework.

3️⃣ Impacts ​

💡 Political Impact ​

  • The Prime Minister plays a central role in governance, acting as a mediator between the President and the National Assembly. As Acting President, they become pivotal in maintaining constitutional order and national stability.

💡 Economic Impact ​

  • The Prime Minister’s coordination of economic policies directly affects national stability and growth. Key fiscal and economic measures are executed through the Deputy Prime Minister for Economic Affairs.

💡 Social Impact ​

  • Decisions made by the Prime Minister significantly influence social conflict resolution and the overall direction of national policies.

4️⃣ Comparison with Related Terms ​

🔎 President ​

  • The head of state who oversees all aspects of governance, including foreign affairs and national security. Unlike the Prime Minister, the President is an independent, supreme leader.

🔎 Deputy Prime Minister ​

  • Reports to the Prime Minister and focuses on specific areas such as the economy or social affairs, coordinating policies in their designated field.

🔎 Ministers ​

  • Heads of individual ministries who manage their departments under the Prime Minister’s direction.
