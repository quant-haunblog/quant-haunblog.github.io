🧑‍🏫 Resolution for the Repeal of Martial Law ​

Social Feed | 2024.12.16

📌 President Yoon's Martial Law Declaration and Impeachment: Decisive Moments ​

The second impeachment motion against President Yoon Seok-yeol was passed in the National Assembly on the 14th, marking a major turning point in his political journey. Starting as a prosecutor and eventually becoming president, he gained support from the conservative faction with his image as a strong prosecutor who "does not serve any person." However, his declaration of martial law, constitutional controversies related to it, extreme confrontations with the opposition party, and divisions within the ruling party led to his impeachment. The martial law situation ended just 6 hours after the National Assembly passed the motion for its repeal, which was considered a political misstep. Additionally, the obsessive focus on "fraudulent elections" was pointed out as a key reason that triggered the declaration of martial law.

1️⃣ Definition ​

The resolution for the repeal of martial law is the procedure where the National Assembly proposes and approves the lifting of martial law through a vote. Even if martial law was declared to respond to national chaos or an emergency situation, the legislative body can request its repeal if it is deemed unnecessary. This is one of the means by which the legislature checks the abuse of power by the executive.

2️⃣ Principles and Legal Framework ​

The resolution for the repeal of martial law is a procedural control mechanism based on the constitution and related laws.

Article 77, Section 5 of the Constitution

  • If the president declares martial law, the National Assembly can request its repeal with the approval of a majority of its members. The president must comply with this request.

Article 12 of the Martial Law Act

  • The National Assembly has the right to request the repeal of martial law, and if it does so, the president must immediately lift it.

Democratic Control Principle

  • Martial law temporarily restricts constitutional rights in an emergency, but its continuation must be decided by the National Assembly, which holds democratic legitimacy. This is an important means of preventing the abuse of power and upholding the separation of powers.

Legislative Checks and Balances

  • When the executive branch declares martial law, the legislative branch reviews it and, if necessary, requests its repeal, maintaining the balance of power. This procedure is intended to protect citizens' freedoms and rights, which are at the core of democracy.

3️⃣ Implications ​

When the resolution for the repeal of martial law is passed, martial law is immediately lifted, and military control returns to normal. This restores citizens' basic rights and returns the legal order to normal. On the other hand, if the resolution is ignored, it could lead to escalating conflict between the executive and legislative branches, potentially causing a constitutional crisis. Additionally, the process of demanding the repeal of martial law could amplify political confusion or social conflicts.

4️⃣ Comparison with Related Terms ​

  • Martial Law Declaration
    A measure where the president mobilizes troops to maintain order and transfers administrative and judicial powers to the military in times of war, disaster, or national emergency.
  • Emergency Orders
    The president's power to issue orders with the same effect as laws during a national emergency without the need for the National Assembly's approval.
  • National Assembly Plenary Vote
    The procedure where the National Assembly votes on a bill or proposal to make a final decision. The resolution for the repeal of martial law is confirmed through a plenary vote.
