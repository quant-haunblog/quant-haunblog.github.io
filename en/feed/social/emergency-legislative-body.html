🧑‍🏫 Emergency Legislative Body ​

Today's Social Headlines | 2025.01.05

📌 “Emergency Legislative Body” Plan: Allegations and Controversies Surrounding the December 3 Treason Incident ​

Following the declaration of martial law on December 3 last year, plans for an "emergency legislative body" by President Yoon Suk-yeol and related figures have sparked significant controversy. According to the prosecution's investigation, this body was designed to neutralize the National Assembly and seize independent legislative power. As legal disputes over the application of treason charges continue, this issue remains a focal point of investigation and trial proceedings.

1️⃣ Definition ​

An emergency legislative body is a special organization established to replace or supplement existing legislative bodies (e.g., the National Assembly) during a national emergency. While it is generally formed based on legal grounds, any attempt to unilaterally establish such a body under the pretext of martial law could be considered an act of treason.

2️⃣ Principles and Legal Framework ​

📘 Constitution Article 76 ​

  • The President may declare martial law, but this authority is strictly limited to preserving the constitutional order and the rule of law.

📘 Martial Law Act ​

  • Even under martial law, legislative power remains with the National Assembly. Any attempt by the martial law command to infringe upon or replace this power is illegal.

📘 Criminal Code Article 87 ​

  • Treason charges apply to acts of rebellion intended to overthrow state power or disrupt the constitutional order. Establishing an emergency legislative body in violation of this provision may constitute an act of treason.

The emergency legislative body, aimed at neutralizing the National Assembly and exercising independent legislative power, clearly violates the Constitution and the Martial Law Act. The legislative authority of the National Assembly is constitutionally guaranteed as the representative institution of the people, and any act infringing upon this authority constitutes a grave crime against the constitutional order.

3️⃣ Impacts ​

If an emergency legislative body had been established, it could have triggered significant social, political, and legal consequences.

💡 Political Chaos ​

  • Efforts to replace legislative authority would be viewed as abuse of power, undermining the foundation of democratic decision-making.

💡 Social Distrust ​

  • Citizens' fundamental rights could be infringed upon, weakening government legitimacy and causing political turmoil and societal unrest.

💡 Legal Accountability ​

  • Those involved would face serious criminal charges, including treason, potentially prolonging the state of national emergency.

4️⃣ Comparison with Related Terms ​

🔎 Martial Law Act ​

  • A law that temporarily expands military and administrative authority during a state of martial law, while still recognizing the authority of the National Assembly, unlike an emergency legislative body.

🔎 Provisional National Assembly ​

  • A constitutionally grounded institution convened to maintain legislative power during national emergencies, differing from an emergency legislative body in its legality and legitimacy.

🔎 National Security Council (NSC) ​

  • A consultative body advising the President during security crises, differing from an emergency legislative body in that it does not exercise legislative authority.
