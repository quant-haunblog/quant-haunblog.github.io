📰 Minutes of the State Council ​

Social News | 2024.12.19

📌 Constitutional Court Orders Submission of Martial Law Proclamation and State Council Minutes ​

The Constitutional Court has ordered President Yoon Suk-yeol to submit the first Martial Law Proclamation and the Minutes of the State Council by December 24 regarding the December 3 martial law emergency. This is a measure to secure critical evidence for the impeachment trial.

The Minutes of the State Council are necessary to review whether the martial law declaration followed lawful procedures under the Constitution and laws. The Court plans to proceed with the trial even if the President refuses to receive the documents, considering them delivered.

1️⃣ Definition ​

The Minutes of the State Council are official documents recording the discussions and decisions made during State Council meetings. These records serve to systematically manage the government's policy decision-making process and are used as reference materials for follow-up actions. They document the deliberation and resolution processes of major government policies, bills, and presidential decrees, ensuring transparency and fulfilling the public's right to know.

Key Roles ​

Key Roles of The Minutes of the State Council

  • Policy Deliberation: Recording the deliberation process of major policies to ensure their legitimacy.
  • Bill Review: Reviewing and documenting the contents of bills before their submission to the National Assembly.
  • Preservation of Decisions: Maintaining historical records of the nation's major meetings.
  • Enhancing Administrative Transparency: Building public trust by ensuring clarity in decision-making processes.

2️⃣ Principles and Legal Framework ​

The creation and preservation of the Minutes of the State Council are mandated by the Constitution and relevant laws.

📕 Constitution Articles 88 and 89 ​

  • The State Council is defined as the President's advisory body, and it is stipulated that major policies must go through the Council's deliberation.
  • The State Council is the highest decision-making body for government policies, and its minutes are defined as documents recording these deliberation processes.

📕 State Council Regulations Article 12 ​

  • The Minutes of the State Council must record the proceedings and resolutions of meetings and be signed and preserved by the President and Council members.

📕 Public Records Management Act Article 17 ​

  • Major governmental meeting records must be preserved as public records.

📕 Confidentiality Clause ​

  • Matters that may significantly impact national security, diplomacy, or public order can be kept confidential.
  • However, excessive confidentiality may lead to criticism of government opacity and conflicts between protecting state secrets and the public's right to know.

The Minutes of the State Council ensure transparency and procedural legitimacy in decision-making, particularly during national emergencies or major decisions.
If State Council proceedings are bypassed or records are omitted, procedural flaws may arise, potentially rendering decisions unconstitutional.

3️⃣ Impacts ​

The Minutes of the State Council are vital for verifying the legality and transparency of government policy decisions. They ensure compliance with legal procedures and establish the legitimacy of policy implementation. Missing or falsified minutes could result in policy invalidation, accountability measures for those responsible, and a decline in public trust.

In impeachment trials, the Minutes of the State Council can serve as critical evidence for assessing the legality of martial law declarations.

Importance of Minutes

  • Building Public Trust: Public access to the minutes strengthens trust by clearly communicating government decision-making processes.
  • Improving Administrative Efficiency: Documented minutes facilitate follow-up actions and policy consistency.
  • Preventing Legal Disputes: Minutes serve as foundational evidence to prevent administrative and legal disputes.

4️⃣ Comparison with Related Terms ​

🔎 Martial Law Proclamation ​

  • An order issued by the martial law commander during a state of martial law, detailing the scope of application and regulatory provisions.

🔎 Meeting Minutes ​

  • General documents recording the proceedings of governmental or institutional meetings, with less legal authority than the Minutes of the State Council.

🔎 Presidential Work Log ​

  • Documents recording the President's official schedule and major activities, used to understand the policy direction of the nation's leader.
