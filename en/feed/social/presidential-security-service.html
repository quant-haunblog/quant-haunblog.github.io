Protection of the President and Key Figures

The most critical role of the Presidential Security Service is to protect the President and their family. This includes not only the President's official activities at home and abroad but also their private schedules. Key activities include:

  • Presidential Security: Ensuring safety in all areas, including the President's travel routes, event venues, and residence.
  • Family Protection: The President's family is also considered a vital element of national security and is included in the protection scope.
  • Protection of Former Presidents: Providing protection for former presidents for a specified period and additional support when necessary.

Protection of Key National Facilities

The Presidential Security Service is also responsible for maintaining the security of key facilities used by the President. This includes the Presidential Office, the Blue House, and national event venues.

  • Facility Safety Inspections: Conducting thorough pre-checks such as explosives detection and security system evaluations.
  • Controlled Area Management: Preventing unauthorized access through entry control and authorization.
  • Emergency Response: Establishing systems to respond immediately to crisis situations.

Security for International Events and Foreign Dignitaries

As part of international cooperation and diplomacy, the Presidential Security Service handles security for international events and foreign visitors, representing the nation's image.

  • International Summits: Ensuring the safety of leaders at major international conferences such as the G20 and APEC.
  • State Visits: Providing security support during official visits of foreign leaders and high-ranking officials.
  • Event Management: Maintaining order at large-scale national events.

Information Gathering and Analysis

To ensure security, identifying potential risks and developing response strategies in advance is essential. The Presidential Security Service achieves this through its information collection and analysis capabilities.

  • Risk Assessment: Analyzing potential threats and incorporating them into security plans.
  • Technology Development: Leveraging the latest security technologies to minimize risks.
  • Collaborative Networks: Securing comprehensive information by collaborating with domestic and international intelligence agencies and law enforcement.
