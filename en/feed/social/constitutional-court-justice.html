A Constitutional Court Justice is a judge in the Constitutional Court who deliberates and rules on constitutional cases. The court consists of nine justices, who handle critical issues such as presidential impeachment trials, the constitutionality of laws, and party dissolution rulings. Justices are appointed through a structure where the President, the National Assembly, and the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court each recommend three candidates, with the President finalizing the appointments.

Key Roles â€‹

Key Roles

  • Constitutional Complaint Adjudication: Resolving constitutional complaints filed by citizens claiming violations of fundamental rights.
  • Constitutionality of Laws: Determining whether laws enacted by the National Assembly violate the Constitution.
  • Jurisdictional Disputes: Mediating disputes over authority between state institutions.
  • Impeachment Trials: Adjudicating impeachment proceedings against high-ranking officials, including the President.
  • Party Dissolution Rulings: Deciding whether a political party's objectives or activities violate the Constitution and if it should be dissolved.
