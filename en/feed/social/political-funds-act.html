The Political Funds Act is a law that regulates the donation and use of funds necessary for political activities and election campaigns while preventing illegal acceptance and donation of political funds. The Act was established to ensure transparency and fairness in political funds, prevent corruption, and specify the procedures and standards for politicians and political parties to lawfully raise and use funds.

Key Roles of The Political Funds Act

  • Managing Political Activity Funds: Legally managing funds needed for political activities and election campaigns to ensure transparency.
  • Preventing Illegal Funds: Blocking politicians or parties from illegally receiving or using funds.
  • Maintaining Public Trust: Preventing corruption to increase public confidence in the political process.
  • Ensuring Policy Fairness: Preventing unlawful political intervention by specific groups or corporations to support equitable policy-making.

The Political Funds Act serves as an essential legal framework to ensure fairness in political activities and prevent corruption. The law mandates transparency in the sources and uses of funds and imposes strict penalties for violations.

üìï Political Funds Act Key Articles ‚Äã
