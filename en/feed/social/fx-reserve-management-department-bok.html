🧑‍🏫 Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division of the Bank of Korea ​

Social News | 2024.12.31

📌 “U.S. High Interest Rates to Persist, Strong Dollar Expected Next Year: BoK Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division Issues Warning” ​

The Bank of Korea's Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division has predicted that the U.S. Federal Reserve's prolonged high-interest-rate policy and the Trump administration's second-term tariff policies will likely maintain a strong dollar next year. The division cited the relative strength of the U.S. economy and the slowdown in major exporting nations as key factors driving the strong dollar. While the risk of a foreign exchange crisis remains low, the Bank of Korea continues to supply liquidity through repurchase agreements (RPs) to stabilize the market.

1️⃣ Definition ​

The Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division is a division under the Bank of Korea, responsible for managing and investing South Korea’s foreign exchange reserves while supporting domestic and international financial market stability and foreign exchange policy. Its roles include managing and investing foreign exchange reserves, ensuring market stability, and devising strategies to respond to foreign exchange crises. Key responsibilities include.

⚙️ Management of Foreign Exchange Reserves ​

Stable Management, Liquidity Maintenance, Portfolio Diversification

  • Stable Management: Ensures the stability of foreign exchange reserves to secure the stability of the foreign exchange market during economic crises.
  • Liquidity Maintenance: Maintains liquidity to enable immediate use in international financial markets and domestic foreign exchange markets when needed.
  • Portfolio Diversification: Develops and implements diversified investment strategies considering exchange rate fluctuations, interest rate changes, and investment risks.

⚙️ Safe and Profitable Investments ​

Prioritizing Safety, Enhancing Profitability, Risk Management

  • Prioritizing Safety: Foreign exchange reserves are critical assets for national economic stability, and their safety is given the highest priority.
  • Enhancing Profitability: While maintaining stability, the division seeks to maximize long-term returns by investing in various assets such as government bonds, corporate bonds, equities, and derivatives.
  • Risk Management: Systematically analyzes portfolio risk factors and develops mitigation strategies.

⚙️ Exchange Rate and Market Stability ​

Market Intervention and Maintaining International Credibility

  • Market Intervention: Stabilizes exchange rates by utilizing foreign exchange reserves during excessive market volatility.
  • Maintaining International Credibility: Enhances South Korea’s economic credibility on the global stage through stable foreign exchange reserve management.

⚙️ International Financial Cooperation ​

Collaboration with International Organizations and Nations

  • Collaboration with IMF and International Organizations: Utilizes foreign exchange reserves in cooperation with international financial institutions or as part of joint responses.
  • Bilateral Financial Cooperation: Supports currency swap agreements with key nations and contributes to global financial stability.

⚙️ Research and Analysis ​

Monitoring Financial Trends and Anticipating Risks

  • Monitoring Financial Trends: Conducts research on global economic and financial market changes to inform reserve management strategies.
  • Anticipating Risks: Identifies potential risks to the profitability and stability of foreign exchange reserves and prepares countermeasures in advance.

⚙️ Transparency and Accountability ​

Disclosure, Reporting, and Compliance

  • Disclosure and Reporting: Transparently discloses the status and performance of foreign exchange reserve management to earn public and market trust.
  • Compliance and Audit: Ensures compliance with laws and regulations while undergoing continuous audits to validate proper reserve management.

2️⃣ Principles and Legal Framework ​

📖 Management of Foreign Exchange Reserves ​

  • Foreign exchange reserves are critical assets for maintaining economic stability and creditworthiness. The division ensures their safe and efficient management.
  • During heightened market volatility, the division intervenes by supplying dollars or implementing liquidity measures to stabilize exchange rates.
  • Investments primarily focus on safe assets such as government bonds, corporate bonds, and deposits, considering liquidity and profitability based on international standards.

📖 Bank of Korea Act ​

  • Article 99 of the Bank of Korea Act mandates the management and operation of foreign exchange reserves, authorizing active intervention during national emergencies like foreign exchange crises.
  • The Foreign Exchange Transaction Act specifies the Bank of Korea’s role as a participant in the foreign exchange market, supporting stabilization measures.
  • The Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division manages reserves and collaborates with international financial institutions to prevent and respond to foreign exchange crises.

3️⃣ Impacts ​

The Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division’s operations directly influence South Korea’s economic stability and creditworthiness. Stable foreign exchange markets reduce the volatility of the Korean won and mitigate risks for export and import businesses. Additionally, the size and management of foreign exchange reserves are key factors in international credit ratings, impacting the nation’s creditworthiness.

4️⃣ Comparison with Related Terms ​

🔎 Bank of Korea ​

  • South Korea’s central bank, under which the Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division specializes in foreign exchange management and reserve operations.

🔎 Ministry of Economy and Finance ​

  • Oversees overall foreign exchange policy, while the management and operation of reserves fall under the Foreign Exchange Reserve Management Division.

🔎 International Monetary Fund (IMF) ​

  • Focuses on stabilizing member countries' foreign exchange markets and providing financial support, similar to the division’s stabilization efforts on a national scale.
