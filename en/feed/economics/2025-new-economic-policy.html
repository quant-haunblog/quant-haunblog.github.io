Overview of 2025 Policy Changes in Korea ​

Economics Feed | 2024.12.31

Tomorrow marks the beginning of 2025. From finance to childbirth, childcare, and real estate, significant changes are coming in various sectors. Here is a quick overview of the policies that will change in 2025.

💡 Quick Highlights

  • Starting next year, financial benefits will be expanded, including a minimum wage of 10,030 KRW, reduced mortgage prepayment penalties, increased contributions for Youth Leap Accounts, and a raised deposit insurance limit of up to 100 million KRW.
  • Benefits for childbirth and childcare will improve, with a shortened working hours system, higher parental leave pay, shared parental leave for couples, and support for pregnancy and childbirth medical expenses.
  • Real estate policies will ease, such as relaxed restrictions on rebuilding apartments over 30 years old, benefits for purchasing unsold homes outside the metropolitan area, the introduction of the Youth Housing Dream Loan, and relaxed income requirements for newborn special loans.

1️⃣ Financial Policy Changes in 2025 ​

💰 Minimum Wage of 10,030 KRW ​

  • The minimum wage will increase from this year’s 9,860 KRW to 10,030 KRW. In monthly terms, based on a standard of 209 working hours per month (40 hours per week), this amounts to a minimum monthly wage of 2,096,270 KRW.

🏦 Reduced Mortgage Prepayment Penalties ​

  • From tomorrow (January 1), prepayment penalties for mortgages from major commercial banks will be reduced by 50%. This will lower the barriers for borrowers to refinance at lower rates.

👨‍🎓 Increased Contributions for Youth Leap Accounts ​

  • Government contributions for Youth Leap Accounts will increase to a maximum of 33,000 KRW per month. This enhancement allows participants to achieve an annual return of up to 9.54%.

🛡️ Deposit Insurance Limit Raised to 100 Million KRW ​

  • The deposit insurance limit will be increased from 50 million KRW to 100 million KRW, providing greater stability for depositors.

3️⃣ Stress DSR Stage 3 Implementation ​

  • From July 1, 2025, Stage 3 of the Stress Debt Service Ratio (DSR) will be implemented. With an added interest rate margin of at least 1.5 percentage points compared to Stage 2, borrowing limits are expected to tighten further.

💡 Understanding Stress DSR

  • The Debt Service Ratio (DSR) measures the ratio of a borrower’s income consumed by debt repayment. Stress DSR includes a higher interest rate to account for potential increases in repayment burdens, effectively reducing borrowing limits.

2️⃣ Childbirth and Childcare Benefits Changes in 2025 ​

⏰ Shortened Working Hours System ​

  • The period during which pregnant workers can apply for reduced working hours will expand. Starting February 23, 2025, women in their first 12 weeks or after their 32nd week of pregnancy can apply, extending the coverage by four weeks.

👩‍🍼 Higher Parental Leave Pay ​

  • From next year, parental leave pay will increase to a maximum of 2.5 million KRW per month. The first 1–3 months will be capped at 2.5 million KRW, 4–6 months at 2 million KRW, and beyond 7 months at 1.6 million KRW. Immediate payment of benefits will replace the previous system of deferred payment after six months.

👩‍❤️‍👨 Shared Parental Leave ​

  • Couples can now share parental leave for up to three years, with each parent eligible for up to 1.5 years starting February 23, 2025. Applications for parental leave can now be integrated with maternity leave requests.

👶 Support for Childbirth and Children ​

  • Pregnancy and childbirth medical vouchers remain at 1 million KRW per child. The First Meeting Voucher (childbirth support grant) will continue at 2 million KRW for the first child and 3 million KRW for subsequent children. Monthly child benefits of 100,000 KRW will now cover children under eight years old, up from seven.

3️⃣ Real Estate Policy Changes in 2025 ​

🏠 Relaxed Restrictions on Rebuilding Apartments Over 30 Years Old ​

  • From June 2025, apartments older than 30 years can undergo reconstruction without requiring safety inspections, lowering the threshold for redevelopment.

🏘️ Benefits for Purchasing Unsold Homes Outside the Metropolitan Area ​

  • Purchases of unsold homes outside metropolitan areas will not count toward a buyer’s total property holdings, allowing them to benefit from tax exemptions on acquisition, capital gains, and comprehensive real estate taxes.

👫 Introduction of Youth Housing Dream Loan ​

  • Youth Housing Dream Loans, designed to help young people purchase homes or finance rental deposits at low interest rates, will be introduced in 2025. Youth with annual incomes under 70 million KRW and no home ownership can borrow up to 80% of the purchase price at a 2.2% interest rate.

👶 Relaxed Income Requirements for Newborn Special Loans ​

  • Starting next year, income requirements for newborn special loans will be relaxed. Households with combined incomes of up to 250 million KRW will be eligible, and families with newborns during the loan term will benefit from additional interest rate reductions of up to 0.4%.
