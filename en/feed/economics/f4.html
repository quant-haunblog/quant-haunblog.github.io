During the recent December 3 martial law crisis and impeachment turmoil, the F4 (Finance4) meeting drew significant attention. Commonly referred to as the "Macroeconomic and Financial Issues Meeting," F4 is the highest-level decision-making body for Korea's economic, monetary, and financial authorities, involving the Minister of Economy and Finance, the Governor of the Bank of Korea, the Chair of the Financial Services Commission, and the Chief of the Financial Supervisory Service. Their swift response during the crisis is credited with stabilizing financial markets.

The Ministry of Economy and Finance (MOEF), Bank of Korea (BOK), Financial Services Commission (FSC), and Financial Supervisory Service (FSS) play central roles in stabilizing Korea's financial system and responding to crises through fiscal policies, monetary policies, financial regulation, and supervision.
