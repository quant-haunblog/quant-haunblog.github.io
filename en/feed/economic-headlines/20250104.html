Today's Economic Headlines | 2025.01.04

📌 South Korea's Stock Market Takes the Lead… Foreign Investors Pile In ​

During the first two trading days of the year, the KOSDAQ index recorded the highest growth rate among major global stock markets. This was driven by perceptions of undervaluation in key domestic stocks and expectations that political instability would ease following the Corruption Investigation Office for High-ranking Officials' attempt to arrest President Yoon Suk-yeol. Additionally, large-scale foreign net buying led the rally, with top market-cap stocks in sectors such as secondary batteries, semiconductors, and biotech performing strongly.

📓 Economic Terms ​

1️⃣ KOSDAQ Index ​

  • A composite stock price index for stocks traded on KOSDAQ, South Korea's over-the-counter market.
  • It plays a crucial role in tracking market trends for small and venture-capitalized companies.

2️⃣ Undervalued ​

  • A condition where a stock or asset's market price is lower than its intrinsic value.
  • It provides potential profit opportunities for investors.

3️⃣ Buying Momentum ​

  • A surge in demand to buy stocks, which supports or pushes stock prices higher.
  • Strong buying momentum reinforces a bullish market trend.

4️⃣ Price-to-Book Ratio (PBR) ​

  • A ratio that divides a stock’s price by its book value (net assets). A PBR below 1 indicates that the stock is undervalued relative to its assets.

💡 Easy-to-Understand Explanation ​

1️⃣ KOSDAQ Index ​

  • The KOSDAQ index shows the average price of stocks traded on the KOSDAQ market as a single number.
  • For instance, if the KOSDAQ index rises, it indicates that many companies listed on the KOSDAQ market are experiencing a general increase in stock prices.

2️⃣ Undervalued ​

  • Undervalued stocks are like “discounted items” on sale. For example, a product originally priced at $100 being sold for $50 can be considered undervalued. Similarly, a stock priced lower than its intrinsic value is deemed “undervalued.”

3️⃣ Buying Momentum ​

  • When many people want to buy the same stock, the stock price rises—this phenomenon is called buying momentum. For example, the surge in demand for a popular new smartphone leading to increased prices mirrors this concept.

4️⃣ PBR ​

  • PBR indicates how cheap or expensive a stock is relative to the company’s assets. If the PBR is below 1, the stock is considered undervalued compared to the company’s asset value.

📈 Economic Outlook ​

The KOSDAQ index's top performance among global markets during the new year's opening days is attributed to perceptions of undervaluation in South Korean stocks and hopes for political stability. Foreign investors' active buying, particularly in sectors like secondary batteries, semiconductors, and biotech, further bolstered the rally.

👌 Positive Factors:

  1. Attractiveness of Undervalued Stocks: Undervaluation in key stocks heightened investment appeal.
  2. Foreign Net Buying: Substantial foreign capital inflows supported the market.

⚠️ Risk Factors:

  1. Exchange Rate Fluctuations: High exchange rates could deter foreign investors.
  2. Corporate Earnings Slowdown: Concerns about weaker corporate performance in Q4 of last year may limit the rally.
  3. Political Instability: While expectations for stability have risen, the possibility of renewed instability cannot be ruled out.

Experts believe that the South Korean stock market's undervaluation supports the likelihood of sustained buying momentum. However, stable exchange rates and improved corporate earnings will be necessary for a full-fledged market rally.

Investors are advised to consider long-term economic prospects and political stability rather than relying solely on short-term rebounds when making investment decisions.
