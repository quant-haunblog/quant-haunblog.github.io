üìù Introduction to Obsidian, A Tool for Productivity and Knowledge Management ‚Äã

In an era where Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) is becoming increasingly important, various note-taking apps are emerging. Among them, Obsidian stands out as a connection-based note-taking tool.

Obsidian is a cross-platform note-taking app that supports Markdown syntax. Similar to Notion and Roam Research, it is well-suited for implementing methodologies like the Zettelkasten and PARA methods.

1Ô∏è‚É£ Overview of Obsidian ‚Äã

Obsidian is a local file-based Markdown note-taking app. All notes created by the user are saved as Markdown files in local folders, ensuring excellent data ownership and security. It also offers a Graph View that allows users to build a knowledge network through linking between notes and visually understand the relationships between them. This connection-focused structure is effective not only for organizing notes but also for expanding thoughts and building deep knowledge.

For example, while note-taking apps like Evernote or Notion may struggle with managing an increasing number of notes due to a lack of linking concepts, Obsidian provides features such as note linking, tags, and a visual graph view to systematically organize and connect information. These features help users easily navigate complex ideas and information, recognize relationships among them, and think from new perspectives.

Although other note apps like Roam Research, Upnote, and Logseq offer similar concepts, Obsidian stands out with its customizable flexibility, local file-based data management, and a rich plugin ecosystem.

2Ô∏è‚É£ History of Obsidian ‚Äã

Obsidian was launched in 2020 by two developers, Shida Li and Erica Xu. They aimed to create a connection-based knowledge management tool that existing note apps could not provide.

Since its launch, Obsidian has rapidly expanded its features by reflecting diverse user feedback and has gained significant popularity, especially among developers and professional communities. Currently, it is available for free for personal use and also offers paid plans for teams and businesses.

Manifesto on Obsidian's Core Principles

‚ë† Markdown-Based Writing ‚Äã

  • You can quickly and efficiently take notes using the simple and intuitive Markdown syntax. Even without the Obsidian app, documents can be read and edited anywhere with any text editor, making migration to other note-taking platforms exceptionally easy.
  • Additionally, since Markdown is purely text-based, Obsidian offers faster performance than any WYSIWYG note-taking app.

‚ë° Local Data Storage ‚Äã

  • Since all notes are stored locally, you can use Obsidian without an internet connection, ensuring high security for personal data. It operates locally, eliminating the need for an internet connection or separate cloud storage, and there are no policy-imposed limits on the number of documents or storage capacity.

‚ë¢ Bidirectional Linking ‚Äã

  • Notes can be mutually linked, allowing users to easily find related information and expand the context of their knowledge.

‚ë£ Graph View ‚Äã

  • It visually displays the connections between notes, enabling users to understand their knowledge network at a glance. This feature allows users to easily create mind maps and diagrams based on their documents.
  • In other words, it supports both markup-based document editing and efficient visualization.

‚ë§ Plugin Extensibility ‚Äã

  • Users can freely expand Obsidian's functionality through numerous official and community plugins. Examples include calendars, task managers, and PDF viewers.
  • Essential features are already available as core plugins. Common tools like the file explorer tab, search function, graph view, and backlinks are all built-in plugins. There‚Äôs no need to search for similar community plugins, and if you prefer a lightweight setup, you can disable any unused core plugins.

‚ë• Multi-Platform Support ‚Äã

  • Obsidian is available on Windows, macOS, Linux, and even mobile apps for Android and iOS, allowing you to write and manage notes anytime, anywhere. Additionally, with the paid feature Obsidian Sync (cross-device note synchronization), real-time synchronization across platforms is possible.
